MacHelper Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 13, 2026

MacHelper is a local-first macOS menu-bar utility for display controls Finder context menu actions, and local Safe encryption.

MacHelper is the Mac App Store edition of the product family. Updates are handled through the Mac App Store.

No app account No MacHelper sign-in or user profile.
No analytics No event tracking, session tracking, advertising pixels, or usage telemetry.
Local actions Display, Finder, and Safe features run on your Mac.

Data Collection

MacHelper does not collect, store, sell, share, or transmit personal information through its display-management, Finder action, or Safe encryption features. Xintech LLC does not receive information about your displays, files, settings, usage, or device from the app unless you intentionally send it through email support.

  • No MacHelper account is required.
  • No analytics, tracking SDKs, advertising SDKs, or crash-reporting SDKs are included.
  • No background telemetry is sent to Xintech LLC.

Local Data

MacHelper may store a small amount of local preference data and bounded local diagnostic logs on your Mac. This can include:

  • Menu settings such as auto blackout, mirroring, display adjustment, keyboard shortcut, and start-at-login preferences.
  • Finder context menu preferences, including which actions are enabled and which editor, terminal, or compatible app you selected.
  • Folder access bookmarks that remember folders you approved for Finder actions, so macOS does not ask again for those folders.
  • Safe workflow preferences such as the last destination you chose through a macOS save or open panel. MacHelper never stores Safe passwords.
  • Local diagnostic messages used to troubleshoot display and Finder-extension behavior.

These files stay on your Mac unless you choose to send them to support.

Display Controls

MacHelper uses macOS display APIs to detect displays, mirror or unmirror displays, darken the built-in screen, dim displays until key press, and adjust display or keyboard brightness where supported. It does not send display information to Xintech LLC.

Finder Context Menus

MacHelper includes a bundled Finder extension. If you enable it in macOS settings, Finder may show MacHelper actions for selected files, folders, or Finder windows.

  • MacHelper reads the current Finder selection only when you choose a MacHelper Finder action.
  • New File creates an empty text file in the current Finder folder, after you approve access to that folder.
  • New Folder creates an untitled folder in the current Finder folder, after you approve access to that folder.
  • Paste copies Finder clipboard items into the current Finder folder, after you approve access to that folder.
  • Copy Path writes the selected path to the clipboard on your Mac.
  • Open actions pass the selected path to the local app you chose, such as TextEdit, Terminal, Codex, Claude, or VSCode.
  • Encrypt to New Safe reads the selected local files or folders and writes a single encrypted .safe file after you choose a password and destination.
  • Add to Existing Safe reads the selected local items and the chosen .safe file, then rewrites a replacement Safe after the operation succeeds.
  • Decrypt Safe reads the chosen .safe file and restores its contents only to the folder you choose.
  • Finder action preferences may be mirrored through the app group shared by MacHelper and its Finder extension.

MacHelper does not upload Finder paths or file contents to Xintech LLC. Apps you open from a Finder action may have their own privacy policies.

Safe Encryption

Safe encryption and decryption are local actions. A .safe file is an encrypted archive created from the files or folders you selected. The archive manifest and file contents are encrypted locally, and passwords are never stored, logged, uploaded, or sent to Xintech LLC.

Decryption writes to a temporary staging folder first and moves the completed result into the destination only after the full operation succeeds. If an operation fails or is canceled, MacHelper removes the temporary output when possible.

Permissions

macOS may ask you to enable the Finder extension or approve access needed to open selected files or folders in another app. New File, New Folder, Paste, Open, and Safe actions may ask for file or folder access when macOS requires this sandboxed app to confirm access. Approving a folder also covers its subfolders, and the approval is remembered on your Mac. These approvals are managed by macOS. MacHelper cannot grant itself those permissions.

Feedback and Support

If you choose Report a Bug, Suggest a Feature, or Send Feedback, MacHelper opens an email draft. You can review, edit, or delete the email and any included diagnostic text before sending it.

Contact

For privacy questions or support, contact admin@xintechllc.com.
